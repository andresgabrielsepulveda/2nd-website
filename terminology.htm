Exhibition Drill

A slightly indepth guide to a dangerous and fun sport

Terminology:

Before I go into a whole mess of words that beginners may not understand, here is a list of several words I might use throughout the website and what they mean. Don't worry if you don't know most of them, you'll hopefully understand them in at least a basic context by the end of this guide.

Air Riflery
Refers to a team that uses rifles discharged by compressed air in various competitions.
Cadet
Someone who is enrolled in JROTC or ROTC (except for Navy ROTC, where they are referred to as Midshipman).
Color Guard
Can pertain to the Military, Police, or JROTC/ROTC. Refers to a unit that performs various ceremonies involving the American Flag, as well as other flags that can vary from unit/service. Not to be confused with a Marching Band Color Guard, who throw flags and rifles to the sound of a marching band. Throwing rifles/flags in a "Military" Color Guard is extremely disrespectful.
Daisy
Commonly used to refer to model rifles made by Daisy Outdoor Products.
Demil
Short for Demilitarized. Commonly used to refer to rifles that have had their mechanisms adjusted so they can't fire.
DrillAmerica™
Commonly refers to rifles created by DrillAmerica™.
Drill Team
Can pertain to the Military, Police, or JROTC/ROTC. Refers to a team of service members/police officers/cadets/midshipmen who perform Regulation and/or Exhibition drill in varying capacities. Drill Teams perform either unarmed or armed with either rifles or in rare cases sabers/swords.
Exhibition Drill
Term used to group all forms of performances involving either military drill movements or rifles as either a team or in a solo performance. It's traditionally performed in varying capacities by JROTC units, ROTC units, Military Drill Teams/Honor Guards, Police/Firemen Honor Guards, etc.
Honor Guard
Can pertain to the Military, Police, or JROTC/ROTC. Refers to a team that performs anything from firing parties, exhibition drill, or a number of ceremonies for their unit/town. Generally, Honor Guards at the Military/Police level tend to perform firing parties and other ceremonies relating to funerals, while JROTC/ROTC units tend to use the term to wrap a Drill Team, Color Guard, and/or Saber Team all into one unit.
Junior ROTC (JROTC)
High school level leadership development programs that utilize military standards and traditions to develop students into quality leaders. Usually, these units are one per highschool, and host extra-curricular activities such as Drill Team, Color Guard, Air Riflery, etc. Members of this program do not incur any military commitment. There are JROTC units for every service all over the nation, except for the Coast Guard.
M1
Nickname for the M1 Garand rifle.
Midshipman
The equivelant to a cadet, this refers to a person who is enrolled in Navy ROTC.
Regulation Drill
Drill as performed based on specific guidelines set forth by either a military service or other governing body.
ROTC
College level leadership program designed to prepare college students to comission in the military as a 2nd Lieutenant (Or Ensign in the Navy). Once a cadet/midshipman graduates from their university and completes their training in ROTC, they do incur a military commitment that varies depending on several factors. There are ROTC units for the Army, Air Force, and Navy, and the Marines fall under Navy ROTC.
Saber Team
Usually pertains to a JROTC/ROTC unit. Refers to a team that performs various functions handling a saber/sword.
Springfield(s)
Nickname for the M1903 Springfield rifle.
Transfer
Commonly used when describing two or more members of a Drill Team/Honor Guard exchanging rifles.
Unservicable
Usually used to refer to rifles that are in a state that renders them extremely dangerous to perform with, and more specifically they cannot be repaired to restore them to a performance-ready state.
